Our Method

Design Process

Inspiration

Every design begins with inspiration drawn from architecture, nature, and cultural elements. We study light patterns, shadow play, and geometric relationships.

1

Conceptualization

Ideas transform into sketches and 3D models. We explore proportions, material relationships, and lighting performance through digital prototyping.

Prototyping

Physical prototypes test form, function, and finish. We refine details, validate electrical performance, and ensure manufacturing feasibility.

2

Refinement

Iterative testing perfects every detail. We adjust proportions, optimize light distribution, and fine-tune manufacturing processes.

Production

Master craftsmen bring designs to life using traditional metalworking techniques combined with modern precision tools and quality control.

3

Finishing

Each piece receives meticulous finishing. Brass is hand-polished, patinas are carefully applied, and every fixture is individually inspected.

Artisan Skills

Master Craftsmanship

Hand Forging

Traditional metalworking techniques shape brass and steel with hammer and anvil, creating unique textures and character impossible through machine production.

Precision Machining

Computer-controlled mills and lathes achieve tolerances measured in thousandths of an inch, ensuring perfect component fit and electrical safety.

Welding Artistry

Master welders create invisible joints that enhance both structural integrity and aesthetic beauty, with seams that disappear into the design.

Finish Application

Chemical patinas, hand-polishing, and protective coatings are applied using techniques passed down through generations of metalworkers.
