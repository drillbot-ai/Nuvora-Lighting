Current Trends

2025 Design Trends

Warm Metallics

Brass and bronze finishes bring warmth and luxury back to contemporary spaces, moving away from cool chrome and steel.

Geometric Forms

Bold geometric shapes and architectural lines create sculptural lighting that serves as functional art in modern interiors.

Mixed Materials

Combining metals with glass, wood, and stone creates rich textural contrast and adds visual depth to lighting design.

Statement Scale

Oversized fixtures make bold statements, while clustered smaller pieces create dramatic impact through repetition and grouping.

Expert Advice

Design Tips

1

Layer Your Lighting

Combine ambient, task, and accent lighting for functional versatility and visual interest throughout the day.

2

Scale Appropriately

Choose fixture sizes that complement room proportions - larger spaces need substantial pieces for proper visual weight.

3

Consider Finish Cohesion

Coordinate metal finishes throughout connected spaces while allowing for intentional contrast in feature areas.

4

Mind the Height

Hang dining fixtures 30-36 inches above table surfaces, and ensure adequate clearance in walkways.

5

Plan for Dimming

Install compatible dimmer switches to control ambiance and adapt lighting for different activities and times.

6

Embrace Patina

Natural brass develops beautiful patina over time - consider this living finish as part of your design evolution.
