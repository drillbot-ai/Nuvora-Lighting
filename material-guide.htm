4

Natural Curing

Master glassblowers shape molten glass using breath and gravity, creating unique forms with optical precision and organic beauty.

1

Furnace Gathering

Gather molten glass at 2000°F onto blowing iron with precise temperature control.

2

Form Shaping

3

Optical Refinement

Polish and refine surfaces for optimal light transmission and clarity.

4

Annealing

Controlled cooling process relieves stress and ensures long-term stability.

Material Care

Care Guidelines

Plaster Care

  • Dust gently with soft brush
  • Avoid water exposure
  • Allow natural breathing
  • Touch up with matching plaster

Alabaster Care

  • Clean with damp microfiber cloth
  • Use pH-neutral stone cleaner
  • Avoid abrasive cleaners
  • Polish annually with stone wax

Wood Care

  • Oil treatment every 6 months
  • Dust with microfiber cloth
  • Maintain consistent humidity
  • Sand and re-oil if needed

Glass Care

  • Clean with glass cleaner
  • Use lint-free cloths
  • Check mounting security
  • Handle with care during cleaning

Concrete Care

  • Seal annually with penetrating sealer
  • Clean with mild alkaline cleaner
  • Avoid acidic cleaners
  • Allow natural weathering patina

Brass Care

  • Polish monthly with brass cleaner
  • Embrace natural patina development
  • Apply protective wax coating
  • Buff gently with soft cloth
