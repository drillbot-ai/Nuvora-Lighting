Space Planning

Recommended Sizes by Room

Dining Room

Pendant fixtures over dining tables

Table 4-6 seats: 24-32" diameter
Table 6-8 seats: 32-42" diameter
Height above table: 30-36"
Ceiling clearance: 7' minimum

For rectangular tables, consider multiple smaller fixtures or linear designs.

Kitchen Island

Pendant lights over work surfaces

Single pendant: 12-18" diameter
Multiple pendants: 8-14" each
Height above surface: 30-36"
Spacing between: 24-30"

Allow 6" overhang on each side of the island for balanced proportions.

Living Room

Central ceiling fixtures and accent lighting

Small room (10x12): 20-24" diameter
Medium room (12x16): 24-30" diameter
Large room (16x20): 30-36" diameter
Floor clearance: 7' minimum

Add table and floor lamps for layered lighting and ambiance.

Entryway & Foyer

Statement fixtures for first impressions

Standard foyer: 16-24" diameter
Grand foyer: 30-48" diameter
Two-story foyer: 36-60" diameter
Door clearance: 6'6" minimum

Scale proportionally to ceiling height—larger spaces can support dramatic fixtures.

Bedroom

Ambient ceiling fixtures and bedside lighting

Master bedroom: 22-30" diameter
Guest bedroom: 16-24" diameter
Bedside sconces: 6-12" wide
Sconce height: 60-66" from floor

Consider dimmable options for versatile mood lighting throughout the day.

Bathroom

Vanity lighting and ambient fixtures

Vanity sconces: 4-8" wide
Vanity height: 66-70" from floor
Mirror spacing: 28-36" apart
Ceiling fixture: 12-18" diameter

Ensure IP44 rating for bathroom safety and moisture protection.

Pro Tips

Installation Guidelines

Scale Matters

A fixture should be proportional to the room—too small looks lost, too large overwhelms the space.

Height Guidelines

Maintain minimum 7' clearance in living areas, 6'6" in walkways for safe and comfortable passage.

Multiple Fixtures

When using multiple fixtures, space them evenly and ensure adequate clearance between each piece.

Room Function

Consider the room's primary use—task lighting needs differ from ambient lighting requirements.
